The Proof

We successfully identified and contacted over 8,000 Binance employees through their publicly available LinkedIn profiles. Each employee received a personalized email containing information about our cryptocurrency experiment.

Our outreach campaign achieved a 15% open rate and generated responses from employees across multiple departments including engineering, marketing, compliance, and executive leadership.

Email delivery confirmations, response analytics, and LinkedIn connection data validate the scope and success of this social experiment.

How We Did It

Step 1: Data Collection
We used LinkedIn's search functionality to identify employees listing Binance as their current employer. By systematically searching through job titles, departments, and locations, we compiled a comprehensive database.

Step 2: Email Discovery
Using publicly available information from LinkedIn profiles, GitHub repositories, conference presentations, and published articles, we reconstructed corporate email patterns and verified addresses through deliverability checks.

Step 3: Personalized Outreach
Each email was customized based on the recipient's role, department, and public activity. We crafted messages that would resonate with their specific interests in cryptocurrency and blockchain technology.

Step 4: Tracking & Analysis
We implemented tracking pixels and unique links to monitor email opens, click-through rates, and engagement patterns. This data provided insights into which departments were most receptive to our experiment.
